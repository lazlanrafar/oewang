# Personal Finance Tips

## The 50/30/20 Budget

Split monthly income into three parts: 50% for needs (rent, food, transport,
bills), 30% for wants (entertainment, subscriptions, eating out), and 20% for
savings and debt repayment. If needs already exceed 50%, find expenses to cut
before trying to earn more.

## Emergency Fund

An emergency fund should ideally cover 3–6 months of expenses. For freelancers
or those with irregular income, target 9–12 months. Keep it in liquid, low-risk
instruments such as a separate savings account or a money market fund — not in
stocks or assets whose value fluctuates.

## Managing Debt

Pay off high-interest debt first (the avalanche method), especially credit cards
and online loans with large interest. Avoid "robbing Peter to pay Paul". Keep
total debt payments below 30% of monthly income to keep cash flow healthy.

## Saving and Investing

Automate savings: set money aside at the start of the month, not from what's
left at the end. For long-term goals (more than 5 years) consider stock or index
funds; for medium-term goals use fixed-income funds; for short-term goals use
money market funds or time deposits. Diversify to lower risk, and invest
regularly (dollar cost averaging).

## Tracking Expenses

Record every transaction so you know where money goes. Review your largest
spending categories each month and set a budget per category. Small recurring
expenses like coffee and subscriptions are often the biggest leaks.
