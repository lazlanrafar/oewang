# Indonesian Income Tax Basics

> Note: This is general educational information, not official tax advice.
> Rates and rules can change; refer to the official DGT site (pajak.go.id) or a
> tax consultant for final decisions.

## NPWP (Taxpayer ID)

An NPWP (Nomor Pokok Wajib Pajak) is required for individuals who earn income.
Since 2024, the NIK on the ID card (KTP) functions as the NPWP for individual
taxpayers.

## PTKP (Non-Taxable Income)

PTKP is the income threshold that is not taxed. Annual amounts:
- Individual taxpayer: IDR 54,000,000
- Additional for married status: IDR 4,500,000
- Additional per dependent (max 3): IDR 4,500,000

Annual net income below the PTKP is not subject to income tax.

## Progressive Income Tax Rates (Article 17)

Applied to Taxable Income (net income minus PTKP):
- 0 – IDR 60,000,000: 5%
- IDR 60,000,000 – IDR 250,000,000: 15%
- IDR 250,000,000 – IDR 500,000,000: 25%
- IDR 500,000,000 – IDR 5,000,000,000: 30%
- above IDR 5,000,000,000: 35%

The rates are tiered: only the portion of income within each bracket is taxed at
that bracket's rate.

## MSMEs and 0.5% Final Tax (Gov. Reg. 55/2022)

Micro, small, and medium enterprises with gross turnover up to IDR 4.8 billion
per year may use a 0.5% final income tax on gross turnover. Individual MSMEs get
a turnover allowance of up to IDR 500,000,000 per year that is not taxed — the
0.5% applies only to turnover above that threshold. The 0.5% scheme for
individuals applies for a maximum of 7 years.

## Annual Tax Return (SPT)

Individual taxpayers file their Annual Tax Return by March 31 of the following
year; corporate taxpayers by April 30. Filing is done online via e-Filing on DGT
Online.
